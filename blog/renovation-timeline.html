Home Renovation Timeline: What to Expect from Start to Finish

May 25, 2026 9 min read Renovation Guide, Planning

Planning a renovation is exciting, but many homeowners don't know what to expect once the project begins. Understanding each stage of the renovation process helps reduce stress, avoid surprises, and ensure the project runs smoothly.

Whether you're updating a single room or renovating an entire property, having a clear timeline allows you to plan your schedule and prepare your home before work begins.

In this guide, we'll walk through the typical renovation process from the first consultation to the final inspection.

Step 1: Initial Consultation

1

Initial Consultation

Every successful renovation begins with a conversation.

During the first meeting, you'll discuss:

  • Your renovation goals
  • Budget
  • Timeline
  • Materials
  • Design preferences
  • Existing wall conditions

This is also the perfect opportunity to ask questions and receive professional recommendations.

A free on-site estimate allows the contractor to inspect the project in person and provide an accurate quotation.

Step 2: Measurements and Planning

2

Measurements and Planning

Accurate measurements are essential before ordering materials.

During this stage, the contractor will:

  • Measure walls
  • Inspect ceilings
  • Check drywall condition
  • Identify repairs
  • Confirm material quantities

If wallpaper or custom wall panels are being installed, precise measurements help prevent unnecessary material waste.

Step 3: Choosing Materials

3

Choosing Materials

Once measurements are complete, it's time to select materials.

Depending on your project, this may include:

  • Wallpaper
  • Wall panels
  • Paint colors
  • Drywall products
  • Trim
  • Decorative mouldings

Taking time to choose the right materials ensures the finished space reflects your personal style.

Step 4: Ordering Materials

4

Ordering Materials

Some products are available immediately, while others require several weeks for delivery.

Custom wallpaper, decorative wall panels, and specialty materials often have longer lead times.

Ordering materials early helps avoid unnecessary project delays.

Step 5: Preparing the Space

5

Preparing the Space

Before work begins, the area should be prepared.

Preparation usually includes:

  • Moving furniture
  • Protecting floors
  • Covering nearby surfaces
  • Removing artwork
  • Disconnecting electronics if necessary

A clean workspace allows the renovation to proceed more efficiently.

Step 6: Surface Preparation

6

Surface Preparation

This is one of the most important stages of any renovation.

Depending on the project, preparation may include:

  • Drywall repair
  • Hole patching
  • Sanding
  • Surface cleaning
  • Priming
  • Removing old wallpaper

Skipping preparation almost always affects the final appearance.

Professional contractors spend significant time ensuring walls are properly prepared before installation or painting begins.

Step 7: Installation or Painting

7

Installation or Painting

Once surfaces are ready, the main renovation work begins.

This may include:

  • Wallpaper installation
  • Wall panel installation
  • Interior painting
  • Exterior painting
  • Drywall installation
  • Decorative feature walls

Each project follows a slightly different process, but attention to detail remains the same.

Professional installation ensures smooth finishes, accurate alignment, and clean workmanship.

Step 8: Quality Inspection

8

Quality Inspection

Before considering the project complete, every detail should be inspected.

The contractor checks:

  • Paint consistency
  • Wallpaper seams
  • Panel alignment
  • Trim details
  • Overall finish

Small touch-ups are completed before final approval.

Step 9: Cleanup

9

Cleanup

A professional renovation should leave your home clean.

Cleanup typically includes:

  • Removing protective coverings
  • Vacuuming dust
  • Collecting construction debris
  • Cleaning work areas

The goal is for homeowners to enjoy the finished space immediately.

Step 10: Final Walkthrough

10

Final Walkthrough

The final walkthrough allows homeowners to review the completed project with the contractor.

During this stage you can:

  • Inspect workmanship
  • Ask questions
  • Request minor adjustments if needed
  • Confirm project completion

This final inspection ensures complete customer satisfaction.

Typical Renovation Timeline

Every project is different, but here's a general guide.

Wallpaper Installation

Most feature walls can be completed within one day after wall preparation.

Interior Painting

A standard room usually takes one to two days depending on preparation requirements.

Drywall Repair

Small repairs are often completed in one day, while larger projects require additional drying time.

Wall Panel Installation

Most decorative feature walls can be completed within one day, although custom designs may require additional time.

Full Room Renovation

A complete room renovation may take several days depending on the scope of work and material availability.

How Homeowners Can Help

You can help your renovation run smoothly by:

  • Removing fragile items
  • Providing easy access to the work area
  • Confirming material selections early
  • Asking questions before work begins
  • Keeping communication open throughout the project

Good communication helps prevent misunderstandings and keeps the project on schedule.

Why Professional Planning Matters

Experienced contractors don't simply complete the work—they manage the entire renovation process.

Professional planning helps:

  • Reduce delays
  • Improve efficiency
  • Prevent material shortages
  • Maintain quality
  • Deliver consistent results

A well-organized renovation saves both time and money.

Final Thoughts

Every successful renovation follows a clear process.

From the initial estimate and material selection to installation, cleanup, and the final walkthrough, each stage plays an important role in achieving outstanding results.

Understanding the renovation timeline allows homeowners to prepare with confidence and enjoy a smoother, more organized experience.

Whether you're installing wallpaper, upgrading with decorative wall panels, painting your home, or repairing drywall, working with an experienced contractor ensures your project is completed professionally and with attention to every detail.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical renovation take?

It depends on the size of the project. Small feature walls may be completed in a day, while larger renovations can take several days or weeks.

Should materials be ordered before scheduling installation?

Yes. Scheduling is much easier once all materials have arrived and are ready for installation.

Can homeowners stay in the house during renovations?

In most cases, yes. This depends on the size of the project and the work being performed.
