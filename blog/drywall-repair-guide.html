Drywall Repair: How to Fix Holes, Cracks, and Water Damage

July 29, 2026 8 min read Drywall, Repair Guide

Drywall is one of the most common wall materials found in homes and commercial buildings throughout Toronto. Although it is durable, everyday wear and tear can eventually lead to damage that affects both the appearance and structural integrity of your walls.

Whether you're dealing with a small nail hole, a large dent, or water damage from a leak, repairing drywall properly is essential before painting or installing wallpaper.

This guide explains the most common drywall problems, how they are repaired, and when it's best to call a professional.

Common Types of Drywall Damage

Drywall damage comes in many forms, but the most common issues include:

  • Nail holes
  • Screw holes
  • Dents
  • Furniture damage
  • Door handle impacts
  • Cracks
  • Water damage
  • Large holes
  • Corner bead damage

Each type of damage requires a different repair method.

Small Holes

Nail holes, screw holes, and small dents

Medium Holes

Furniture damage, door handle impacts

Large Holes

Major impacts requiring drywall replacement

Cracks

Settling, temperature changes, structural movement

Water Damage

Leaks, roof damage, plumbing issues

Corner Damage

Damaged corner bead from impacts

Repairing Small Holes

Small holes left by nails or picture hooks are the easiest drywall repairs.

1

Clean the Area

Remove any loose debris around the hole.

2

Fill the Hole

Fill the hole with drywall compound using a putty knife.

3

Allow to Dry

Let the compound fully dry before sanding.

4

Sand Smooth

Sand the area until it is level with the surrounding wall.

5

Apply Primer

Apply primer to the repaired area to seal the surface.

6

Paint

Paint the repaired area to match the rest of the wall.

When completed properly, the repair becomes virtually invisible.

Repairing Medium-Sized Holes

Larger holes caused by furniture or accidental impacts require additional reinforcement.

Professional repairs often involve:

  • Removing loose drywall
  • Installing a drywall patch
  • Applying drywall tape
  • Multiple coats of joint compound
  • Sanding between coats
  • Priming and painting

Proper layering creates a repair that blends seamlessly into the surrounding wall.

Repairing Large Holes

Large drywall holes usually require replacing part of the drywall panel.

This process includes:

  • Cutting out the damaged section
  • Installing new drywall
  • Securing the panel to framing
  • Taping the joints
  • Applying multiple coats of joint compound
  • Sanding
  • Priming
  • Painting

Large repairs require precision to ensure the finished wall appears continuous.

Fixing Drywall Cracks

Cracks commonly appear because of:

  • Normal house settling
  • Seasonal temperature changes
  • Structural movement
  • Poor previous repairs

Simply applying more compound over a crack often results in the crack returning.

Professional repairs usually include:

  • Opening the crack slightly
  • Applying drywall tape
  • Multiple coats of compound
  • Sanding
  • Priming
  • Painting

This creates a much stronger repair.

Repairing Water Damage

Water damage should always be investigated before cosmetic repairs begin.

Common causes include:

  • Roof leaks
  • Plumbing leaks
  • Window leaks
  • Basement moisture

The moisture source must be corrected first.

Once the area is completely dry, damaged drywall can be removed and replaced.

Ignoring moisture problems often leads to recurring damage and mold growth.

Repairing Damaged Corners

Outside drywall corners frequently become damaged by:

  • Furniture
  • Moving boxes
  • Everyday traffic

Corner repairs may involve replacing the damaged metal or vinyl corner bead before applying fresh joint compound.

Professional corner repairs restore clean, straight edges.

Why Proper Sanding Matters

Sanding is one of the most important parts of drywall repair.

Proper sanding removes:

  • High spots
  • Tool marks
  • Uneven compound
  • Surface imperfections

Smooth walls are essential before painting or wallpaper installation.

The Importance of Primer

Many homeowners skip primer after drywall repairs.

This is a mistake.

Primer helps:

  • Seal repaired areas
  • Create even paint absorption
  • Improve paint adhesion
  • Prevent flashing

Without primer, repaired areas often remain visible after painting.

Common DIY Mistakes

Many drywall repairs fail because of simple mistakes.

Common problems include:

  • Applying too much compound
  • Skipping drywall tape
  • Poor sanding
  • Painting without primer
  • Rushing drying times
  • Using the wrong patch materials

Professional repairs avoid these issues.

Can Wallpaper Be Installed Over Repaired Drywall?

Yes.

However, wallpaper requires smooth walls.

Before wallpaper installation, repaired areas should be:

  • Fully cured
  • Smoothly sanded
  • Properly primed
  • Free from dust

Proper preparation ensures invisible seams and long-lasting adhesion.

When Should You Hire a Professional?

Small nail holes are often manageable for homeowners.

Professional drywall repair is recommended when dealing with:

  • Large holes
  • Water damage
  • Ceiling repairs
  • Multiple damaged areas
  • Cracks that continue to return
  • Wallpaper preparation
  • High-end paint finishes

Professional repairs produce smoother, longer-lasting results.

Preventing Future Damage

To keep drywall looking its best:

  • Repair leaks immediately
  • Avoid excessive wall impacts
  • Maintain proper indoor humidity
  • Use quality paint
  • Repair small damage before it becomes larger

Regular maintenance helps extend the life of your walls.

Final Thoughts

Drywall repair may appear simple, but achieving invisible results requires careful preparation, quality materials, and attention to detail.

Whether you're repairing a few nail holes or replacing water-damaged drywall, proper repairs create a smooth foundation for painting, wallpaper installation, or decorative wall panels.

Investing in professional drywall repair ensures your walls remain beautiful, durable, and ready for years of everyday use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can drywall be repaired without replacing the entire wall?

Yes. Most drywall damage can be repaired by replacing only the damaged section.

What causes drywall cracks?

The most common causes are normal house settling, temperature changes, and structural movement.

Can water-damaged drywall be repaired?

If the drywall has lost its structural integrity or mold is present, replacement is usually the best solution after fixing the source of the leak.

How long does drywall repair take?

Small repairs may be completed in one day, while larger repairs often require additional drying time between coats of joint compound.
