How to Prepare Walls for Wallpaper Installation: A Complete Guide

July 8, 2026 8 min read Wallpaper, Preparation Guide

Beautiful wallpaper starts long before the first strip is installed. One of the biggest factors that determines whether wallpaper looks flawless—or fails prematurely—is proper wall preparation.

Even the highest-quality wallpaper can develop bubbles, visible seams, or poor adhesion if installed on an unprepared surface.

Whether you're installing wallpaper in a living room, bedroom, office, or commercial space, preparing the walls correctly is the foundation of a successful installation.

This guide explains every step professional installers follow to create smooth, durable, and beautiful wallpaper finishes.

Why Wall Preparation Is So Important

Wallpaper is only as good as the surface beneath it.

Poor wall preparation can lead to:

  • Peeling edges
  • Air bubbles
  • Visible wall imperfections
  • Loose seams
  • Poor adhesive performance
  • Shorter wallpaper lifespan

Professional preparation helps ensure the wallpaper looks seamless and lasts for many years.

Step 1: Remove Old Wallpaper

1

Remove Old Wallpaper

If wallpaper is already installed, it should be completely removed before installing new material.

Removing old wallpaper may require:

  • Warm water
  • Wallpaper remover
  • Steam remover
  • Scrapers
  • Patience

All remaining adhesive should also be removed.

Installing wallpaper over existing wallpaper is generally not recommended because imperfections can become visible through the new material.

Step 2: Inspect the Walls

2

Inspect the Walls

Once the walls are exposed, carefully inspect the entire surface.

Look for:

  • Nail holes
  • Drywall damage
  • Dents
  • Cracks
  • Loose joint compound
  • Water damage
  • Uneven surfaces

Small imperfections often become much more noticeable after wallpaper is installed.

Step 3: Repair Drywall Damage

3

Repair Drywall Damage

Before wallpaper installation, all damaged areas should be repaired.

Professional repairs may include:

  • Filling nail holes
  • Patching drywall
  • Repairing corner damage
  • Replacing damaged drywall sections
  • Applying joint compound

Proper repairs create a smooth foundation for wallpaper.

Step 4: Sand the Surface

4

Sand the Surface

Sanding removes rough spots and creates an even surface.

Professional installers sand:

  • Drywall repairs
  • Joint compound
  • Previous paint drips
  • Surface imperfections

Smooth walls produce smoother wallpaper.

Step 5: Clean the Walls

5

Clean the Walls

Dust, grease, and dirt prevent wallpaper adhesive from bonding properly.

Walls should be thoroughly cleaned before installation.

Cleaning removes:

  • Sanding dust
  • Oils
  • Dirt
  • Construction debris

The surface should be completely dry before moving to the next step.

Step 6: Apply Primer

6

Apply Primer

Primer is one of the most important steps in wallpaper installation.

A quality wallpaper primer:

  • Improves adhesion
  • Creates an even surface
  • Makes future wallpaper removal easier
  • Prevents excessive adhesive absorption
  • Helps wallpaper slide into position during installation

Skipping primer often leads to poor installation results.

Step 7: Allow the Primer to Dry

7

Allow the Primer to Dry

Do not rush this step.

Primer must fully dry before wallpaper installation begins.

Following the manufacturer's recommended drying time ensures maximum adhesive performance.

Different Wall Types Require Different Preparation

New Drywall

Fresh drywall should always be finished, sanded, and primed. Wallpaper should never be installed directly onto bare drywall.

Painted Walls

Previously painted walls are usually excellent candidates for wallpaper. However, they should still be cleaned, sanded if glossy, and primed when necessary.

Textured Walls

Wallpaper performs best on smooth walls. Heavy textures should usually be skim-coated and sanded before wallpaper installation.

Common Wall Preparation Mistakes

Many wallpaper problems are caused by skipping preparation.

Common mistakes include:

  • Installing over dirty walls
  • Ignoring drywall repairs
  • Skipping primer
  • Installing over glossy paint without sanding
  • Leaving dust on the surface
  • Rushing the drying process

Professional preparation prevents these issues before installation even begins.

Why Professional Preparation Matters

Experienced wallpaper installers understand that preparation often takes nearly as much time as the installation itself.

Professional preparation creates:

  • Smooth surfaces
  • Better adhesion
  • Cleaner seams
  • Longer-lasting results
  • A premium finished appearance

Investing time in preparation almost always produces better long-term results.

Signs Your Walls Are Ready

Your walls are ready for wallpaper installation when they are:

  • Clean
  • Dry
  • Smooth
  • Properly repaired
  • Fully primed
  • Free of dust
  • Structurally sound

At this point, wallpaper installation can begin with confidence.

Final Thoughts

Preparing walls properly is one of the most important steps in achieving a beautiful wallpaper installation.

Although preparation requires additional time, it ensures better adhesion, smoother seams, and a finish that will last for many years.

Whether you're installing a simple accent wall or a custom mural, never underestimate the importance of proper wall preparation.

Professional preparation is the difference between a good wallpaper installation and an exceptional one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do walls always need primer before wallpaper?

Yes. Professional wallpaper primer improves adhesion, creates a smoother installation surface, and makes future wallpaper removal much easier.

Can wallpaper be installed over textured walls?

Light textures may be acceptable, but heavily textured walls should usually be skim-coated and sanded smooth before installation.

Should drywall repairs be completed before wallpaper?

Absolutely. Every hole, crack, and damaged area should be repaired before wallpaper is installed.

How long should primer dry before wallpaper installation?

Drying time depends on the product used, but installers should always follow the manufacturer's recommendations before applying wallpaper.
